Australian Open

16th-29th January (2023)

Melbourne Park, Victoria, Australia

Hard

ASD 2,875,000 (singles champions)


The Australian Open is the first of the 4 Grand Slam competitions held throughout the year. held every January. It has been held at Melbourne Park since 1998. It has grown to become one the the largest sporting events held in the Southern Hemisphere and is the highest attended Grand Slam tournament, with more than 812,000 people attending the event in 2021.


Reigning Singles Champions

Mens - Rafael Nadal (Spain)

Women's - Ashleigh Barty (Australia)


All-time Greats

Novak Djokovic has the most men's singles championships, with 9 titles between 2008 and 2021, he is therefore considered one of the best hard-court players of all time. This is ahead of Roy Emerson and Roger Federer, who have 6 titles each. Margaret Court is the most prolific female champion with 11 titles. Serena Williams is the most successful player in recent times, winning the event 7 times.


The Australian Open Experience

The Australian Open is held in Melbourne, the capital and largest city of the state of Victoria, and the second-most populous city in both Australia and Oceania. The Australian Open is held in January, which falls in the middle of summer in the Southern Hemisphere, meaning average temperatures remain around 26 degrees Celsius. Melbourne is known as a trendy area, with the city offering exquisite dining and entertainment opportunities for both residents and visitors.

French Open

28th May-11th June (2023)

Stade Roland Garros, Paris, France

Clay

EUR 2,200,000 (singles champions)

The French Open (also known as Roland Garros) is the second of the 4 Grand Slams throughout the year, held every May/June. The tournament and venue are named after the French aviator Roland Garros. It is the premier clay court championship in the world and the only Grand Slam held on this surface. Due to the nature of playing on clay, the French Open is widely regarded as the most physically demanding tennis tournament in the world. Clay courts are considered 'slow' as the tennis balls bounce relatively high and lose much of their speed on contact of the surface, making it more difficult for players to return shots. Movement on clay is also different and more difficult than other surfaces, and play often involves sliding on the loose ground.


Reigning Singles Champions

Mens - Rafael Nadal (Spain)

Women's - Iga Swiatek (Poland)


All-time Greats

Rafael Nadal has the most men's singles championships, with a record 14 titles between 2005 and 2022, he is therefore considered the best clay court player of all time. This is ahead of Bjorn Borg who has 6 titles at the French Open.
Chris Evert is the most prolific female champion with 7 titles. Steffi Graf is a 6-time champion. The current champion, Iga Swiatek, has won the title twice, the first being in 2020 when she was just 19 years old.


The French Open Experience

The French Open is held in Paris, the capital and most populous city of France. Since the 17th century, Paris has been one of the world's major centres of finance, commerce, fashion, food, and science. Paris is a major railway, highway, and air transport hub, with two international airports, a bustling metro system and international railway station in the city centre. The city is also world-renowned for its history, museums, and architectural landmarks. Sports events are also hugely popular, with football and tennis being at the heart of this.
The French Open is held during late spring in Paris, meaning average temperatures remain around 22 degrees Celsius. These factors combined make Paris the perfect destination for tennis fans to visit during the time of the championship.

Â£2,000,000 (singles champions)

The Wimbledon Championships (also known as Wimbledon) is the third of the 4 Grand Slams throughout the year, held every June/July. It is the oldest tennis tournament in the world and globally regarded as the most prestigious. It has been held at the All England Club (link to wiki page) in Wimbledon, south west London since 1877 and played on outdoor grass courts. It has also provided 2 courts with retractable roofs since 2019. The history of Wimbledon holds many traditions including a string all-white dress code for players, royal patronage, and consumption of strawberries and cream. Unlike most other professional tournaments, corporate advertisement and endorsement from sports-based suppliers such as Slazenger, Nike, and Rolex is very minimal.


Reigning Singles Champions

Mens - Novak Djokovic (Serbia)

Womens - Elena Rybakina (Kazakhstan)


All-time Greats

Roger Federer has the most men's singles championships, winning the title a record 8 times, he is therefore considered one of the best grass-court players of all time. This is ahead of Novak Djokovic and Pete Sampras, who have 7 titles each at this event. Martina Navratilova is the most successful female champion with 9 titles at Wimbeldon. Serena Williams and Steffi Graf both hold 7 titles here in second place.


The Wimbledon Experience

Wimbledon is a district of Southwest London, England, situated 7 miles southwest of the centre of the city. It contains Wimbledon common, one of the largest areas of common land in London, and includes a residential and retail area. Tennis fans and tourists flock to London during the summer, with it being the capital and largest city of England in the United Kingdom, with a population of over 9 million spanning the city. London has been a major settlement for over 2,000 years, and therefore offers historical, architectural, and cultural venues, as well as being a major hub and influence in the arts, entertainment, fashion, commerce, finance, education, media, science, and transport. London also has 4 World Heritage Sites, as well as the royal residence of Buckingham Palace, and The Royal Observatory in Greenwich. London is also a major site of large sporting events as well as Wimbledon, including Premier League, the London Marathon, the Summer Olympic Games, as well as International competitions in Rugby, Cricket Athletics, and Rowing.
The Wimbledon Grand Slam is held in mid-summer in the UK, therefore average temperatures are 19 degrees Celsius, creating the perfect conditions for a summer getaway for tennis fans.

US Open

28th August - 10th September (2023)

USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center, New York City,
United States

Hard

US$ 2,600,000 (singles champions)

The US Open is the final of the 4 Grand Slams throughout the year, held every August/September. It is held in Queens, New York, and is owned by the US Tennis Association, a non-profit organisation, and revenue from ticket sales, sponsorships, and television contracts is used to develop tennis in the United States. The ground of the US open have 22 outdoor courts. The main court is the 23,771-seat Arthur Ashe Stadium, which is the largest tennis stadium in the world.


Reigning Singles Champions

Mens - Carlos Alcaraz (Spain)

Women's - Iga Swiatek (Poland)


All-time Greats

Roger Federer, Jimmy Connors, and Pete Sampras have won the event a record 6 times each, leading the field in this championship. This is closely followed by Rafael Nadal and John McEnroe who have both won the event 4 times in their careers, with Nadal still competing professionally. The current champion, Carlos Alcaraz, is one the youngest male Grand Slam champions in history, winning the title at 19 years old. Chris Evert and Serena Williamshave won the event a record 6 times each, followed by Steffi Graf with 5 titles at this event. The current champion, Iga Swiatek, won her 2nd Grand Slam of the year in 2022 by winning the US Open.


The US Open Experience

The US Open is held in the Borough of Queens, New York City, also known as NYC. NYC is the most populous city in the United States, with a population of over 8 million people. It is also the most densely populated major city in the US, and is part of the New York Metropolitan area, the largest metropolitan area in the world by urban landmass. New York City is known globally as a cultural, financial, and media centre with a great influence in commerce, entertainment, education, research, food, art, fashion and sports.
The US Open is held in late summer in New York City, where average temperates at this time of year stand at 23 degrees Celsius, making it a great destination for tennis lovers at this time of year.
