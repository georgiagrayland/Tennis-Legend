The Grand Slam Competitions

Australian Open

16th-29th January (2023)

Melbourne Park, Victoria, Australia

Hard

ASD 2,875,000 (singles champions)

The Australian Open is the first of the 4 Grand Slam competitions held throughout the year. held every January. It has been held at Melbourne Park since 1998. It has grown to become one the the largest sporting events held in the Southern Hemisphere and is the highest attended Grand Slam tournament, with more than 812,000 people attending the event in 2021.

Reigning Singles Champions

Mens - Rafael Nadal (Spain)

Women's - Ashleigh Barty (Australia)

All-time Greats

Novak Djokovic has the most men's singles championships, with 9 titles between 2008 and 2021, he is therefore considered one of the best hard-court players of all time. This is ahead of Roy Emerson and Roger Federer, who have 6 titles each. Margaret Court is the most prolific female champion with 11 titles. Serena Williams is the most successful player in recent times, winning the event 7 times.

The Australian Open Experience

The Australian Open is held in Melbourne, the capital and largest city of the state of Victoria, and the second-most populous city in both Australia and Oceania. The Australian Open is held in January, which falls in the middle of summer in the Southern Hemisphere, meaning average temperatures remain around 26 degrees Celsius. Melbourne is known as a trendy area, with the city offering exquisite dining and entertainment opportunities for both residents and visitors.
